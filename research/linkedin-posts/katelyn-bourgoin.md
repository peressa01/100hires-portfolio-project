## Post 1 — Ownable Ideas
**Date:** recent

Top paid experts all have one thing in common: they sell ideas. Not just time.

And idea owners get rich.

Today Simon Sinek charges $100K+ for a keynote. But before his famous "Start With Why" TED Talk? He was a relatively unknown marketing consultant.

One idea can change everything. Not a product. Not a service. An *idea*.

A well-sculpted idea supported by the right story and shared religiously can build your authority, spread while you sleep, and generate demand (without chasing).

I call these "ownable ideas." And there are 6 types:

**1. Distinctive Philosophy** — A belief about how things should be done that others can live by.
- "Start With Why" (Simon Sinek)
- "Everything is Figureoutable" (Marie Forleo)

**2. New Category** — Defines a new type of solution or market space you can lead.
- "Zero-Click Content" (Amanda Natividad)
- "Dark social" (Chris Walker)

**3. Coined Problem** — Names a struggle people recognize but didn't have words for.
- "Law of Shitty Clickthrough" (Andrew Chen)

## Post 2 — One welcome email question = double revenue
**Date:** recent

One question. One welcome sequence. Double the revenue.

Katelyn was able to double her revenue for her Why We Buy newsletter by simply adding a poll question in her welcome email.

Instead of just saying "Hi thanks for subscribing," she asked: "What's your biggest marketing challenge right now?" with 3 answers as a poll question.

She then created dynamic sequences based on the pain points people selected. Each pain point leads naturally into the product that solves it.

## Post 3 — The Expert's Paradox
**Date:** recent

The best insights are usually the ones people don't think are worth saying.

Katelyn Bourgoin calls it the expert's paradox. When you're too close to your own thinking, brilliance starts to feel like common sense. So it goes unshared.

It's especially true for founders. When you've been building something for years, it's hard to see what's actually worth saying out loud.

That's where marketers come in. Your job isn't just to create content. It's to go find what's already there.

Great marketing teams don't just create content. They go mine it.
