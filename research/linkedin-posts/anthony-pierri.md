"We have our positioning strategy figured out." ...but do you really?

I often start calls with CMOs who say "we have our positioning strategy dialed in." I then spend 10 minutes asking clarifying questions. And then the CMO says, "...I guess we actually do not have this figured out..."

A lot of positioning strategies are actually NON-strategies.

The "decision" they have made is to "not make a decision." If a positioning strategy requires zero tradeoffs, it's unlikely it is a real strategy.

This is a much simpler question-tree that can help you diagnose if your company has a positioning problem (without needing to do the 10 minute back and forth with me directly).

## Post 3

I'm convinced the main group that's raving about replacing every UI with a chat interface is executives.

They are used to just giving orders to people who go and do things for them — why shouldn't the software be that way too?

Why open the Starbucks app to order my drink when I can literally ask ChatGPT to open the app and order it for me? (Nevermind doing it myself would take ~15 seconds, and the AI way takes on average ~1:45)

If you've lived in a boss-everyone-around paradigm and can't be bothered with something as pedestrian as USING A USER INTERFACE, then the chat-only world is very appealing.

## Post 4
**Date:** hace 1 día

I'm just going to say it: Usage-based pricing is less customer-centric than seat-based pricing.

It's great for the COMPANY. But literally no customer wants their pricing to be variable.

The biggest differentiator between OpenAI and Anthropic is that ChatGPT still is a flat rate per month, and Claude has gone full boar on usage-based. It's likely inevitable that Sam will follow suit, but do not be confused: This is a net NEGATIVE for the customer.

You can come at me with "but usage-based is aligned with outcomes!" — If those "outcomes" are going ten rounds to get the AI to move some pixels around or fix a bug that it introduced on its own...it never feels good to read, "Hey you just ran out of tokens!"
