## Post 1

I quit drinking alcohol six years ago.

Here's what my life looked liked then vs. now:

Me in 2019:

> Drinking 2-4 beers/drinks every night
> Getting hammered once a week with friends
> Drinking was 100% of my stress relief AND social life
> Weighed 225 (20lbs heavier than now)
> Looked like I was 45, when I was 39
> Business stuck at $3M ARR for the third year in a row
> Single, chasing, bouncing around the world… lost

Me in 2026:

> Zero booze or drugs of any kind in 5.5 years
> Stress relief is sauna, cold plunge, and wakesurfing
> Social life is hanging out with people who have kids
> Weigh 205 (20lbs lighter)
> Look like I did when I was 34 (and I'm 45)
> Business 10x bigger, personally making 10x more
> Happily married, two young kids, settled in Aspen

If you ask me what the most impactful decision I've made over the past 10 years there is no question what I'd say…

Quitting drinking.

For anybody who drinks every day, if you take three months off, you will notice an enormous difference in the quality of your life.

For me, I'd been drinking so much for so long that when I stopped it felt like I had a superpower.

You'll feel like you got 25% of your life back.
You'll be more present in all of your relationships.
You'll be far more intentional in everything you do.
You'll be much better at home, and at work.

There's really only one thing I regret about the whole situation…

Not quitting sooner.

## Post 3
**Date:** hace 6 días

Marty Kausas: Why do you hate VCs so much?

Me: How much time do you have?

BACKGROUND:

Marty is a young-buck YC-founder with early traction.
Ed Hallen is the co-founder of Klaviyo, an IPO'd decacorn.

Every Marty is hoping to become Ed someday. Ed has reached the endgame of the life the VC/YC path is selling, he's worth $100's of mm now, and I spoke to him the other day about this.

Ed: "Most VC-backed founders end up failing. That just sucks for Founders."

He told me about an article he wrote for the Harvard Business Review about what he calls the "Mighty Middle".

He concluded sub-VC scale, bootstrapped companies in niche markets had far higher probabilities of creating life-changing outcomes for founders than the VC track... And therefore are better for the Founders!

But that wasn't the most interesting part.

When you talk to Ed about Klaviyo, you can tell he doesn't think of himself and his co-founder as any more capable than their VC-backed peers. You get the sense that he believes Klaviyo is where it is because of a few breaks that were totally out of their control.

1. Linking themselves with Shopify when it was a $50m valuation, now it's $150b (a 300x!)
2. Mailchimp got in a fight and killed their integration w/ Shopify in 2018
3. Email is the one positive NRR product in SMB

The last point Ed made on our call was my favorite. "Hundreds of millions of dollars has zero utility beyond a number MUCH smaller."

His point is that most Founders want the freedom to build cool shit, and live the life they want. A huge amount of money in the bank, and the huge company that young founders think they want, can work AGAINST those goals.

Why do I hate VC's so much? Because I want YOU to win. And I want you to be free.
